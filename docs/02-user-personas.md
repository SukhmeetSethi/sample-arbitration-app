# User Personas

## Persona 1: Claimant (Individual / Business)

**Name:** Priya Sharma — SME Owner, Delhi
**Age:** 38 | **Tech comfort:** Medium

**Context:** Priya's logistics company has a ₹45 lakh payment dispute with a vendor. The contract has an arbitration clause. She wants resolution without spending months in court.

**Needs:**
- File arbitration claim online without visiting a lawyer's office repeatedly
- Track case progress and deadlines in one place
- Attend hearings from her office via video call
- Receive the award digitally and enforce it quickly

**Pain points:**
- Doesn't understand procedural steps — needs guided workflows
- Worried about document security and confidentiality
- Frustrated by opaque timelines in traditional arbitration

## Persona 2: Respondent's Advocate

**Name:** Adv. Rajesh Menon — Senior Advocate, Mumbai
**Age:** 52 | **Tech comfort:** Low–Medium

**Context:** Represents corporate clients in commercial arbitration. Handles 15–20 active arbitrations at any time.

**Needs:**
- Centralized dashboard for all active cases
- Easy document filing with version history
- Calendar integration for hearing schedules
- Secure communication channel with tribunal

**Pain points:**
- Juggles multiple platforms (email, courier, physical filing)
- Misses deadlines due to manual tracking
- Needs associates to have controlled access to case files

## Persona 3: Private Arbitrator

**Name:** Justice (Retd.) K. Venkataraman — Former High Court Judge, Chennai
**Age:** 67 | **Tech comfort:** Low

**Context:** Post-retirement, serves as a sole arbitrator in commercial disputes. Wants to expand his practice beyond Chennai.

**Needs:**
- Simple profile page to showcase expertise and availability
- Receive appointment requests from parties / institutions
- Conduct hearings via video with minimal tech friction
- Draft and sign awards digitally
- Manage fee collection without chasing parties

**Pain points:**
- No online presence; relies on word-of-mouth referrals
- Uncomfortable with complex software — needs a very simple UI
- Concerned about the legal validity of digital signatures on awards

## Persona 4: Institutional Arbitration Centre

**Name:** Mumbai Centre for International Arbitration (MCIA)
**Type:** Institution

**Context:** Administers 200+ arbitrations per year. Needs to manage its panel of arbitrators, case allocation, fee schedules, and compliance reporting.

**Needs:**
- White-label or co-branded portal for their cases
- Panel management: onboard, verify, assign arbitrators
- Fee schedule configuration per dispute value slab
- Reporting: case throughput, average resolution time, arbitrator performance
- Integration with their existing case management system via API

**Pain points:**
- Current system is a mix of spreadsheets and email
- No real-time visibility into case status across the panel
- Manual fee calculation and collection

## Persona 5: Government Court Clerk / e-Filing Officer

**Name:** Ramesh Yadav — Court Clerk, Delhi High Court
**Type:** Government employee

**Context:** Processes Section 34 (setting aside) and Section 36 (enforcement) applications related to arbitral awards.

**Needs:**
- Receive digitally signed awards and applications via e-filing integration
- Verify authenticity of digital signatures
- Access case metadata (parties, arbitrator, dispute value, award date)

**Pain points:**
- Currently receives physical copies that need manual data entry
- No standard format for arbitration-related filings
